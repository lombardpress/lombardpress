# 1. The SCTA: Problems and Aspirations
Today the traditional mechanisms of publication scholarly editions of medieval scholastic texts are no longer helping to advance the scientific research. Instead traditional publishing workflows are preventing progress, by preventing aggregation, machine analysis, access, and distribution.
When it is easier to access the manuscripts an incunabula of Gregory of Rimini’s text than the modern critical edition, it is clear that publication is failing its fundamental task of making data publically available for research.
What’s more, the fragmentation of these editions with different publishers, who prepare the data according to proprietary and incompatible data standards means that the creation of an edition requires wasteful redundancy. It also means that the aggregation of analysis of connected content is impossible.

A quick glance around the internet at research groups attempting to create a web presence for scholastic texts reveals that the paradigm of isolation and redundancy is alive and well on the web.

**In sum**: research groups are currently choosing the most inefficient way possible to make data available on the web, while leaving us with results that go barely beyond the capabilities of the printed page.
